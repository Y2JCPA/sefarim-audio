PELE YOETZ — LOVE BETWEEN HUSBAND AND WIFE
==================================================

Love Between Husband and Wife

The love between a husband and wife is a fundamental obligation, and they should have a strong bond of love. Let's start with the husband's love for his wife. Our sages have taught that a man must love his wife as much as he loves himself and honor her even more than himself. However, this love should not interfere with his service to God. The sages warned against excessive conversation with one's wife, as it can lead to neglect of Torah study and ultimately negative consequences. They also advised that following a wife's advice blindly can lead to trouble.

Therefore, a wise person should act with understanding, balancing love and discipline. The primary love should be for the soul, and it is the husband's duty to gently guide his wife in modesty, to avoid gossip, anger, curses, and the misuse of God's name. He should encourage her to be meticulous in observing commandments, especially in prayer, blessings, and keeping the Sabbath. Teaching her ethical teachings and the words of the sages relevant to her will inspire her to be more careful than the man himself.

For a man blessed with a wise and good wife, she is naturally cherished by him. However, if a man has a difficult wife, he faces a great challenge and must strive to maintain peace for the sake of the divine presence. He should remember that even the greatest can face challenges, and he should not let anger overcome him. Instead, he should gently correct her and accept his situation with joy, as a wife is a gift from God. Our sages said that one who has a difficult wife will not see hell, implying that enduring such trials brings great reward.

A man should also work to maintain love so that his children are not born of a hated union. He should not be overly concerned with household expenses, as jealousy can be destructive. Instead, he should honor his household and appease his wife, for blessings come to a home because of the wife. Peace in the home is so important that even God's name can be erased to maintain it.

Part of this love is not to travel far for long periods, as it causes distress to both himself and his wife. If he must be away, he should write letters to maintain the bond and send gifts to lift her spirits. A small gesture can be a great mitzvah.

The obligation is even greater for the wife to love and honor her husband like a king, to fulfill his wishes wholeheartedly. Her goal should be to understand his desires and make them her own, supporting him in his spiritual endeavors. She should speak to him kindly if she needs to guide him and comfort him in times of trouble. She should rise early to assist him in serving God and ensure she does not hinder his spiritual growth.

A wise woman builds her home, and a God-fearing woman is praised. She should be modest, adorned for her husband, and careful with cleanliness and purity, so her husband does not look elsewhere. If she is blessed with a God-fearing husband, she should naturally love and respect him. But if she has a difficult husband, she should still honor him, as God has paired them together.

A woman should be careful not to reveal her husband's faults to others, as this can lead to greater strife. Instead, she should speak to him gently and pray for him, as a woman's prayer is powerful. She should also pray for her children, as their well-being is tied to her own.

Parents should advise their daughters before and after marriage to honor their husbands and his family. This respect brings peace and harmony, and it is a mitzvah to pursue peace, especially when it is challenging. A woman should be modest and respectful, avoiding inappropriate behavior with her husband's relatives, as such actions can have severe consequences.

These are the ways of righteousness for a man and woman who are blessed. Let the wise hear and increase their learning. There is already guidance in the book "Shevet Mussar" by Rabbi Eliyahu HaKohen, which provides further insights into the conduct between husband and wife. May they continue to act with goodness and bring the divine presence into their home.
