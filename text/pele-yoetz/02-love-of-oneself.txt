PELE YOETZ — LOVE OF ONESELF
==================================================

Love of Oneself

It is natural for a person, as designed by the Creator, to love themselves. A person is closest to themselves and loves themselves more than anyone or anything else in the world, because a person would give everything they have to save their own life. However, there is a serious problem caused by the evil inclination (yetzer hara), which blinds a person and makes them like someone who is sick or drunk, unable to distinguish between right and wrong. This leads a person to harm themselves, both physically and spiritually, in ways that no enemy could. This is a great folly. Indeed, it is said that a person only sins when a spirit of foolishness enters them. Sometimes, this foolishness can even lead a person to self-destruction, heaven forbid.

A wise person should understand that they ought to love themselves, both body and soul, and work towards earning a good reward for themselves, so that their soul can delight and their body can live in peace. Logically, a person should love their body more than their wealth. It is better to have a simple meal in peace and rest than to have great wealth with stress and toil. If God has blessed someone with wealth, they should use it to bring comfort to their body and not toil in this world just for the sake of money. If they have wealth, they should use it to benefit their body and be kind to themselves, as long as they do not indulge excessively in wine, meat, delicacies, constant travel, or excessive sexual activity, as these can destroy both body and soul.

Above all, one must guard against anger, as it is said, "Remove anger from your heart and keep evil away from your body." Anyone who does not protect themselves from things harmful to their body is essentially their own enemy and seeks their own harm, and they will be held accountable. If harming others is a sin, how much more so is harming oneself, which is cruel. One must be especially careful in situations where there is even the slightest risk to life. If someone puts themselves in danger for financial gain, it shows they value money more than their life. If a miracle saves them, it diminishes their merits, as they are essentially selling their future rewards for temporary wealth, which is fleeting and perishable.

Therefore, one should not put themselves in dangerous situations, like risky roads or sea and river travel, unless absolutely necessary for their livelihood. They should travel with companions to reduce danger naturally. If possible, they should choose land routes over sea or river routes, which are more dangerous. The greater the danger, the more a miracle will diminish their merits. Thus, any effort or expense to reduce danger is wise and beneficial, and the commandment to "guard your life diligently" will protect and bless them, ensuring they remain whole in body and wealth.

The essence of loving oneself, both body and soul, should stem from love for the Creator. Just as one cares for their tools to keep them clean and intact because they love themselves and want to use them, so too should one care for their body and soul, which are tools to serve before God. This should be the intention behind caring for one's body and soul, and with this mindset, God will assist and support them.
