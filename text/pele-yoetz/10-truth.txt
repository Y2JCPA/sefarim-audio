PELE YOETZ — TRUTH
==================================================

Truth - "Truthful speech stands forever and saves from death," as it is known from the Talmudic saying about a place called Kishtah, where people did not speak lies and no one died before their time. Our sages said that changing one's words is considered as serious as idol worship, as it is written, "I would appear to him as a deceiver." God is truth, His seal is truth, and His Torah is truth. The remnant of Israel will not commit injustice or speak lies, nor will deceitful speech be found in their mouths.

There are many uneducated people who have trained themselves to speak falsehoods, and it has become completely permissible to them. Some lie about trivial matters with no benefit, and about them, it is said, "Woe to those who draw iniquity with cords of falsehood." When lying is done to gain an advantage, such as in measurements or weights, it is not called lying but rather cleverness and diligence. They say that one who does not lie nowadays cannot make a profit. But this is a falsehood, for our sages taught that when Noah entered the ark, falsehood also wanted to enter. Noah told it to find a partner, as all who entered the ark came in pairs. Falsehood found a partner in Loss. Falsehood asked Loss if it would marry it, and Loss agreed on the condition that it would receive all the profits of falsehood. From then on, whatever falsehood gained, Loss took.

Even if the evil inclination (yetzer hara) convinces a person that they will profit greatly from lying, ultimately they will suffer loss, as it is written, "He who gains wealth unjustly will leave it in the midst of his days." In the end, he will be a fool, or the wealth will be kept to his detriment in this world and the next. God does not withhold good from those who walk with integrity.

Lying is detestable to both God and people. A liar is despised and scorned, and the shame of it is evident to all. Even among the nations, some are very careful to speak truthfully and avoid lies. Those who lie in business dealings with non-Jews cause a desecration of God's name, as the non-Jews say that it is known that Jews are liars, deceivers, and unjust. The severity of desecrating God's name is well known, and many prohibitions stem from lying, depending on the situation. The punishment for a habitual liar is that even when they speak the truth, they are not believed.

Truth is the foundation of everything. It is known from the Midrash that there was once a wicked person who repented by accepting upon himself not to lie. Whenever he was about to commit a sin, he would think: if someone asks me, what will I say? If I tell the truth, I will be ashamed and humiliated; if I lie, I will violate my commitment. Thus, he refrained from following the counsel of the wicked and did not stand in the path of sinners.

Given the great value of truth and the disgrace of falsehood and its consequences, a person should strive to educate their children in the trait of truth. They should see that their parents are strict about truth, and they will do the same. Parents should watch over their children to ensure no deceitful speech is found in their mouths. If they do something improper, they should be encouraged to admit the truth, and they will be forgiven that time without punishment, but warned not to repeat it. If they lie, they should be punished severely for the lie, so that the sin of lying becomes grave in their eyes and they are very careful, until it becomes their nature not to lie.

Part of the trait of truth is to be truthful in one's commitments, whether affirming or denying. Besides the prohibition, it is disgraceful to go back on one's word. It is said, "He who punished the generation of the flood and the generation of the dispersion will punish one who does not keep his word." Therefore, one should think carefully before making a commitment to ensure they can fulfill it. If they have spoken, they should keep their word as if they have taken an oath.

Since our holy Torah is a Torah of truth, one must be very careful to study it truthfully, to acknowledge the truth, and to accept the truth from whoever says it, even the smallest of the small. It is said that the acknowledgment of the sages is their praise. One should not turn to falsehoods and deceitful dealings, but let truth guide their way. This is the straight path for all who walk upright, and through this, they will find favor and good understanding in the eyes of God and people.
