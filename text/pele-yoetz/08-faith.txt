PELE YOETZ — FAITH
==================================================

Faith - Faith is the cornerstone of everything. A person who believes in God, whose glory fills the entire world and who watches over all human actions to reward or punish them accordingly, will live by their faith. Such a person will surely be careful to observe all the commandments and their details, will be enthusiastic in performing them, and will strive to avoid any sin. They will be as strong as a lion in serving their Creator and will not be deterred by anything. They will understand that God's presence is always with them, His eyes roam the earth, seeing and hearing everything, and all their deeds are recorded. This awareness will bring them to serve God with complete devotion, in awe, love, and great joy. They will firmly believe that there is a good reward for the righteous according to their deeds and effort, and a bitter punishment for the wicked for every wrongdoing. God's scales of justice are precise, giving each person what they deserve based on their actions, abilities, and efforts.

There is another aspect of faith that leads to many good qualities: believing in God's providence over every detail of the world. Nothing happens without His decree, whether it comes from heaven or through human actions, as there are many agents of God, both for good and for bad. He gives strength to achieve success, as it is not the wise who have bread or the swift who win the race. Therefore, one should not worry about losing money in the service of God or strive to become wealthy through forbidden means. Instead, they should conduct themselves justly according to the Torah, without being overly strict, flattering, hating, bearing grudges, or taking revenge. They should understand that everything comes from God for their ultimate benefit. God is faithful and without injustice, righteous and upright. Everything God does is for the best, and one should accept divine judgment with joy, not resent His rebuke.

The foundation of everything is to believe in all the words of the Torah and the sages. If one struggles to understand, they should attribute it to their own lack of knowledge and limited understanding, but still believe with complete faith that all their words are true and eternal. Consider how great the words of the sages are, who said that the entire Torah is encompassed in the attribute of faith. Many claim to be believers, but their actions contradict their words, and deeds speak louder than words. God will judge those who walk in darkness, punishing them not only for their transgressions but also for their lack of faith. If someone believes yet still does not follow the right path, it is a sign of arrogance and shamelessness.

Believing in God's existence and His providence are among the 613 commandments, and they are constant commandments that can be fulfilled at any moment through remembrance alone. Therefore, it is good for a person to remember this verbally and not forget it in their heart. From time to time, the Jewish servant should remind themselves, "Why do you, my inclination, always pursue me? May God rebuke you, Satan, for I am a Jew, the child of a Jew, and I believe with complete faith in God's constant presence and His detailed providence. He rewards every good thought, word, and deed, and punishes every bad one, even a light conversation or a bad thought. Therefore, I should act in this way, behave in this manner, and be careful to avoid this and that, so that my actions are pure and straightforward in thought, speech, and deed, without any deceit or crookedness, so that my faith is not false. This is how I will act and not change." Such thoughts should be deeply ingrained in one's heart, constantly reflected upon, and explored in depth and breadth to walk the paths of faith. Let one's hands be faithful, proving their commitment as they were in faith. Then, faith will be good, granting merit for eternal life and true rest, with the main goal being to bring pleasure to the One who dwells on high.
