PELE YOETZ — LOVE OF CHILDREN
==================================================

Love of Children

The love for sons and daughters is natural because they are part of us, our own flesh and blood. Even a raven cares for its young. However, we must be careful that this love does not lead us astray, causing us to spoil our children by not disciplining them or by showing excessive affection. As Solomon wisely said, "He who spares the rod hates his son, but he who loves him is diligent to discipline him." The essence of love is the love of the soul, which includes love for oneself, as a child’s righteousness brings merit to the parent. It is written, "Discipline your son, and he will give you rest; he will give delight to your soul."

Therefore, a person should guide their children with open rebuke from a place of love, according to the nature of the child. When they are young, foolishness is bound in their hearts, and the rod of discipline will drive it away. However, a father must be careful not to be cruel and not to strike his child in anger with harsh blows. Instead, he should guide them gently and, if necessary, strike them on the legs, avoiding places that could cause injury, like between the eyes. As they grow older, a rebuke is more effective for the understanding child. It is known that hitting an older child is forbidden, as it can lead them astray. This does not mean only physically mature children, but according to the child's character and temperament. For older children who do not respond to rebuke, a gentle word can break bones.

A father should not embarrass his child in front of others, as this can damage their reputation. It is crucial to heed the advice of our sages, who warned against showing favoritism among children, as jealousy is as cruel as the grave. The general rule is that a father should act in a way that prevents his child from sinning, and if the child wrongs him, he should quickly forgive so the child is not punished because of him. Sometimes, he should pretend not to hear or see, and occasionally, he should set aside his own desires for theirs.

This is a fundamental principle for one who wishes to benefit his own soul and the souls of his children: the main goal is to bring joy to our Father in Heaven. One should strive with all their might to teach their children Torah. How fortunate and pleasant is the lot of one whose children are scholars! He will rejoice in his offspring in this world and the next. At the very least, he should dedicate himself to ensuring his children are not ignorant, as the ignorance of the unlearned is great. There is a way that seems right to a person, but its end is the way of death. An unlearned person cannot fear sin, nor can they be truly pious.

If he cannot ensure all his children become Torah scholars, he should at least have one child who is a scholar, whose profession is Torah study. He should pour out his soul before God, praying that his children become knowledgeable in Torah, with good character and sound understanding. He should even fast at times to ensure his sins do not prevent good from reaching his children. A person must be especially vigilant with his daughters, as their future is uncertain. Therefore, he should keep them away from bad traits, teach them all kinds of women's crafts, and accustom them to modesty and good character. He should advise them on how to behave with their husbands in a way that finds favor with God and their spouse.

Every wise person acts with knowledge, observing the thoughts and schemes of their children. With strength, he should prevent his son from becoming a cunning hunter, keeping him away from falsehood, as truth is the foundation of all. He should strive to keep his son from speaking vulgarity or swearing, and distance him from any hint of impropriety or ugliness, especially from the sins of youth, which require extra care, wisdom, and vigilance. Do not trust your children blindly, thinking they will not follow the counsel of the wicked or stand in the path of sinners, for there is no guardian against immorality. The greater a person is, the greater their evil inclination (yetzer hara). Youth and bad company can lead one astray.

Therefore, one must gather strength to keep their children away from bad influences and strive with all their might to ensure they follow the path of the righteous. This is the essence of the great effort involved in raising children, and according to the effort is the reward. Rejoice in your offspring in this world, and your soul will exult in the world to come. This love should stem from love of God, striving to gather workers for God, for the ultimate fruit of children is that they fulfill the will of their Creator. Otherwise, it is better for them not to exist than to be evil. Therefore, the entire aim in loving one's children should be to fulfill the Creator's commandments and bring joy to the Creator.
